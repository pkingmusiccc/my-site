# Vitracosmetics — BrandScript (StoryBrand 2.0)

> Single source of truth for every line of copy on the site. If a sentence on the site cannot be traced back to this file, rewrite it. Updated 2026-04-23.

---

## Hero (the customer)

A skincare professional in Switzerland: dermatologist, medspa owner, hotel spa director, skincare-boutique buyer, aesthetic clinician, senior cosmetician. They need to **differentiate their treatment menu** with a premium, science-forward, professional-grade line their clients cannot buy on a high street or through Amazon.

They are busy, technically literate, and reputation-sensitive. They do not need the industry explained to them.

## External problem

German-quality professional cosmetics are **hard to source reliably in Switzerland**: inconsistent supply, unclear regulatory status, slow professional support from across a border, and no single Swiss point of contact.

## Internal problem

Fear of staking a hard-won professional reputation on the wrong brand. Frustration with mass-market lines that clients can buy anywhere — lines that commoditise the treatment room.

## Philosophical problem

Clients deserve formulations with integrity: evidence-led, considered, honest. Beauty-industry theatre is over. Professionals who trade on judgement deserve suppliers who behave the same way.

## The guide

### Empathy

> A professional's reputation is built one client at a time. The line on your shelf speaks for you before you do.

### Authority (facts, not adjectives)

- **Exclusive Swiss distributor** for TAO Cosmetics (Germany).
- **Direct manufacturer relationship** — no middle layer, no parallel importer.
- **Swiss logistics and regulatory handling** — Swissmedic / product-notification matters handled in-country.
- **Professional training included** with onboarding.
- TAO itself: **26+ years** operating, **developed and manufactured in Germany**, **dermatologist-collaborated**, EU-regulated, no animal testing, no paraffins or mineral oils.

## The plan (3 steps)

1. **Discover** — browse the TAO range and your professional pricing.
2. **Consult** — speak with our Swiss team. Fit the line to your treatment menu and your clientele.
3. **Launch** — staff training, onboarding, and marketing assets, so the line earns its place in the room from day one.

## Stakes

### Failure (what not partnering looks like)

An undifferentiated shelf. Treatments that feel interchangeable with the spa down the road. Clients drifting to rooms with better lines. A price ceiling you cannot raise because nothing on the tray justifies it.

### Success (what partnering looks like)

A treatment menu that only you can offer in your canton. Higher average ticket. Clients who return because the line is not available online. A supplier who picks up the phone in Swiss working hours.

---

## Identity transformation

From a professional who sells **products** to one trusted for **judgement**. Partners stop competing on price and start being consulted on what works.

---

## Tone rules (applied to every section)

- Short sentences. Active voice. Concrete nouns.
- Write to a colleague, not a customer.
- Superlatives only when a specific fact backs them (year, ingredient, certification).
- Never use the banned words in §10 of CLAUDE.md.

## Section-by-section copy direction

### 1. Nav
Wordmark left. Links: *Products · About · Contact*. Primary CTA right: **Become a partner**.

### 2. Hero
- **Headline options (write three, pick the quietest one at build time):**
  1. *German precision, distributed properly in Switzerland.*
  2. *The TAO range. Held to a Swiss standard of service.*
  3. *A professional skincare line worth putting your name next to.*
- **Sub:** one sentence — who it is for, what they get.
  - Draft: *Exclusive Swiss distribution of TAO Cosmetics for dermatologists, medspas, hotels, and clinics — with training, logistics, and regulatory handling taken care of in-country.*
- **CTAs:** *Become a partner* (primary) · *Download the catalogue* (secondary).

### 3. Stakes
One strip, two sentences. Direct. Name the cost of sameness.

### 4. Value proposition (three concrete partner benefits — not TAO features)
1. **A line your clients cannot buy anywhere else in the canton.** Exclusivity is part of the contract.
2. **One Swiss phone number.** Orders, regulatory questions, and training — all handled locally, in your working hours.
3. **Onboarding that earns the shelf space.** Staff training, treatment protocols, and point-of-sale assets included.

### 5. The guide (Why Vitracosmetics)
Empathy paragraph → authority bullets (the facts from §Authority above). No superlatives.

### 6. Product showcase
Grid of TAO lines with short, functional captions. Group by line (Clean · Light · Dry · Glossy · More · Body · Sun · Man · Epiceutical). Each line gets a one-line description of **what it does for the client**, not only ingredients.

### 7. The plan
Three steps exactly as above: *Discover · Consult · Launch*.

### 8. Explanatory paragraph
One considered block. The long-form case for TAO + Vitracosmetics. Reads like a page from a trade magazine — one voice, one argument, no bullet points.

### 9. Social proof
Partner-type marquee: medspas, dermatology clinics, day spas, hotel spas, boutiques. Real logos where available; refined silhouettes until then.

### 10. Contact / enquiry form
The conversion point. Form is a **section**, not a modal. Headline: *Start a partnership conversation.* No dark patterns, no double-opt-in gymnastics.

### 11. Footer
Wordmark, address placeholder, legal links, language switcher (disabled in v1).
